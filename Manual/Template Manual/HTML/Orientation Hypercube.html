On the Subject of Orientation Hypercube

If the bomb doesn't kill us, a higher-dimensional creature rotating our brains will.

This module houses a four-dimensional hypercube, a nine-segment panel, a six-button keypad, and a large 'Set' button. There is also an eye looking at the hypercube.

To solve the module, use the keypad to input a sequence of rotations which leave the hypercube in the target orientation, as determined by the colours of its faces. Then, press the 'Set' button.

The Hypercube

The "hypercube" on the module is actually a projection of a tesseract into three-dimenisional space. While a cube has six two-dimensional faces, a tesseract has eight three-dimensional faces.

The outer eight segments of the panel to the right of the hypercube can be used to highlight the faces according to the diagram to the right.
